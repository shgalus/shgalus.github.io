18 /**
19  * Mixtures of normal densities.
20  *
21  * Calculates probability density, distribution function, percentage
22  * points and moments of the mixture of normal distributions.
23  *
24  * Let \f$f_i(x; \mu_i, \sigma_i)\f$, \f$i = 1, \ldots, n\f$, \f$n >
25  * 0\f$, be probability density functions of normal distributions
26  * \f$N(\mu_i, \sigma_i^2)\f$, and \f$w_i\f$ be nonnegative weights
27  * summing up to 1. The probability density of the mixture is defined
28  * as \f[ f(x) = \sum_{i = 1}^n w_i f_i(x; \mu_i, \sigma_i). \f]
29  *
30  * The constructor accepts three arguments: \f$w\f$, \f$\mu\f$ and
31  * \f$\sigma\f$. If an argument is invalid, the exception
32  * Normal_mixture::Error is thrown. The function \c p returns values
33  * of the density. The function \c cdf returns values of the
34  * corresponding distribution function \f$F\f$. The function \c
35  * invcdf(p) returns \f$x\f$ such that \f$F(x) = p\f$ if \f$0 < p <
36  * 1\f$ and throws Normal_mixture::Error otherwise. The functions \c
37  * mean, \c sdev, \c skew and \c curt return mean, standard deviation,
38  * coefficient of skewness and coefficient of curtosis of the mixture
39  * distribution. Any of these four functions may throw the exception
40  * Normal_mixture::Error if it comes out that variance of the mixture
41  * distribution is equal to 0.
42  *
43  * \ingroup mathematical_statistics
44  *
45  * \todo In invcdf(double) const choose better initial values for
46  * bisection.
47  *
48  * \implementation
49  *
50  * The implementation is straightforward. To calculate moments, the
51  * first four raw moments \f$m_k\f$ are calculated as \f[ m_k =
52  * \sum_{i = 1}^n w_i M_i^{(k)}, \f] where \f$M_i^{(k)}\f$ is the
53  * \f$k\f$-th moment of \f$N(\mu_i, \sigma_i^2)\f$ (\cite fisz-1969,
54  * page 160)
55  * \f{align*}{
56  * M_i^{(1)} & = \mu_i \\
57  * M_i^{(2)} & = \mu_i^2 + \sigma_i^2 \\
58  * M_i^{(3)} & = \mu_i^3 + 3\mu_i\sigma_i^2 \\
59  * M_i^{(4)} & = \mu_i^4 + 6\mu_i^2\sigma_i^2 + 3\sigma_i^4
60  * \f}
61  * Then the following formulas \cite fisz-1969, page 80, are used to
62  * calculate central moments:
63  * \f{align*}{
64  * \nu_2 & = m_2 - m_1^2 \\
65  * \nu_3 & = m_3 - 3m_1m_2 + 2m_1^3 \\
66  * \nu_4 & = m_4 - 4m_1m_3 + 6m_1^2m_2 - 3m_1^4
67  * \f}
68  * Finally, mean, standard deviation, coefficient of skewness and
69  * coefficient of curtosis are calculated as \f$m_1\f$,
70  * \f$\sqrt{\nu_2}\f$, \f$\nu_3 / (\sqrt{\nu_2})^3\f$ and \f$\nu_4 /
71  * (\sqrt{\nu_2})^4 - 3\f$, respectively.
72  */
