22 /**
23  * \defgroup calendrical_calculations Calendrical calculations
24  *
25  * The class for date and related functions.
26  *
27  * \internal
28  * Implementation
29  * ==============
30  *
31  * Let us extend Gregorian calendar backwards. 1 Jan. 0001 (Monday) is
32  * given the number 1, 2 Jan. 0001 (Tuesday) is given the number 2 and
33  * so on. In general, 1 Jan. of the \f$n\f$-th year, \f$n > 0\f$, is
34  * given the number \f[ f(n) = 1 + 365(n - 1) + \lfloor \frac{n -
35  * 1}{4} \rfloor - \lfloor \frac{n - 1}{100} \rfloor + \lfloor \frac{n
36  * - 1}{400} \rfloor, \f] and the day of the week of the \f$d\f$-th
37  * day may be calculated as \f$d - 7 \lfloor d / 7 \rfloor \f$ (0 ---
38  * Sun., 1 --- Mon. and so on). Employing both inequalities \f$x - 1 <
39  * \lfloor x \rfloor \leq x\f$ to each component of the definition of
40  * \f$f\f$, the following approximation can be found: \f[ c(n - 1) - 1
41  * < f(n) < c(n - 1) + 2, \; n = 1, 2, \ldots \f] where \f$c = 146097
42  * / 400 = 365.2425\f$. Since \f$f(n+1) - f(n) > c - 3 > 0\f$, \f$f\f$
43  * is a strictly increasing function, and for each \f$m = 1, 2, \ldots
44  * \f$ there exists the greatest natural number \f$n\f$ such that
45  * \f$f(n) \leq m < f(n+1)\f$. For \f$m = 1\f$ this number is 1. For
46  * \f$m > 1\f$, the greatest natural \f$n\f$ satisfying the inequality
47  * \f$c(n-1) + 2 \leq m\f$ is \f$N = 1 + \lfloor (m - 2) / c
48  * \rfloor.\f$ On the right-hand of the above inequality we have
49  * \f$f(N) < m.\f$ Since \f[ m < cN + 2 < cN + (c-1) = c(N+1) - 1 <
50  * f(N+2),\f] we have \f$f(N) < m < f(N + 2)\f$. Hence, the one and
51  * only number satisfying \f$f(n) \leq m < f(n + 1)\f$ is one of the
52  * numbers \f$N\f$ and \f$N + 1\f$. For Julian calendar, the reasoning
53  * is analogous.
54  *
55  * The reform of Julian calendar in 1582, which introduced Gregorian
56  * calendar, consisted in modifying the definition of a leap year and
57  * correcting the ten-day error by accepting that Thursday 4 Oct. 1582
58  * (Julian) is followed immediately by Friday 15 Oct. 1582
59  * (Gregorian). The following table illustrates the way of numbering
60  * dates:
61  *
62  * Number | Gregorian calend. | Julian calendar
63  * ------:| ----------------- | -----------------
64  * 1 | Mon. 01 Jan. 0001 | Mon. 03 Jan. 0001
65  * ...... | ................. | .................
66  * 577735 | Thu. 14 Oct. 1582 | Thu. 04 Oct. 1582
67  * 577736 | Fri. 15 Oct. 1582 | Fri. 05 Oct. 1582
68  * ...... | ................. | .................
69  * 710347 | Mon. 12 Nov. 1945 | Mon. 30 Oct. 1945
70  * ...... | ................. | .................
71  * 729755 | Fri. 01 Jan. 1999 | Fri. 19 Dec. 1998
72  *
73  *
74  * The function Date::easter(int) is based on the following program in
75  * Basic, quoted in "Informatyka" 2/1984 after "PPC Calculator
76  * Journal":
77  * \code
78  * 10 rem Program podaje date Wielkanocy wg kalendarza
79  * 20 rem gregorianskiego czyli od roku 1583 wlacznie.
80  * 30 rem Dla dat wczesniejszych (kalendarz julianski)
81  * 40 rem nalezy podstawic:
82  * 50 rem m = 15 oraz p = 6 (patrz linie 130 oraz 150).
83  * 60 input "rok"; y
84  * 80 a = y - int(y / 19) * 19
85  * 90 b = y - int(y / 4) * 4
86  * 100 c = y - int(y / 7) * 7
87  * 110 t = int(y / 100)
88  * 120 p = 4 + t - int(t / 4)
89  * 130 m = p - int(p / 7) * 7
90  * 140 p = 15 + t - int(t / 4) - int((8 * t + 13) / 25)
91  * 150 p = p - int(p / 30) * 30
92  * 160 p = 19 * a + p
93  * 170 d = p - int(p / 30) * 30
94  * 180 if d < 28 then 220
95  * 190 if d = 29 then 210
96  * 200 if a <= 10 then 220
97  * 210 d = d - 1
98  * 220 p = 2 * b + 4 * c - d + m
99  * 230 p = p - int(p / 7) * 7
100  * 240 d = d + p
101  * 250 if d > 9 then 290
102  * 260 d = d + 22
103  * 270 print "Wielkanoc: "; d; "marca "; y
104  * 280 stop
105  * 290 d = d - 9
106  * 300 print "Wielkanoc: "; d; "kwietnia "; y
107  * 310 end
108  * \endcode
109  *
110  * Bibliography
111  * ============
112  *
113  * \cite dershowitz-reingold-1990.
114  *
115  * Jakub Tatarkiewicz, Wielkanoc, Informatyka 2/1984, p. 19.
116  *
117  * \{
118  */
